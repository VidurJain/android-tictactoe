Tic-tac-toe is an ancient pencil-and-paper game for two players, X and O, who take turns marking the spaces in a 3×3 grid. The player who succeeds in placing three respective marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row wins the game.

Tic-Tac-Toe originates back to ancient Egypt and is one of the oldest games known.
Tic Tac Toe can be also called Tic-tac-toe, TTT, noughts and crosses, X's and O's and X and 0 or:
-	Tick-tack-toe, tic-tac-toe, tick-tat-toe, or tit-tat-toe (USA, Canada)
-	 Noughts and crosses or Naughts and crosses (Great Britain, Republic of Ireland, Australia, New Zealand, South Africa)
-	 Exy-Ozys, Xsie-Osies (verbal name only) (Northern Ireland)
-	Xs and Os (Egypt, Republic of Ireland, Canada, Scotland, Zimbabwe)
-	X and O (India)
-	O-X (Mauritius)
